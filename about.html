About Me

When I was younger, I had no idea that I would one day be interested in computers. I knew that, when I didn't know how to do something, I would spend hours trying to research a solution. I knew that I loved going to Micro Center, even though I barely knew what anything did. I knew that my favorite tasks as a church Administrative Assistant were anything computer-related: figuring out how to connect a switch, working with the church database, and eventually learning to help update the parish website. In college, I took at least three career/major quizzes, and I eventually settled on management. A career in IT wasn't even a thought. It wasn't until I took the initiative and completed Rockport that I began to connect the dots: I was actually really interested in computers. I was taking some Java programming and intro IT courses at the time, which further solidifed my interest.

After graduating college, I decided to take a gap year to work full-time and see if this career was right for me. I was very lucky to get a job at a high school's Help Desk, which gave me my first real deep-dive into the world of computing and inspired me to go back for my Masters. After a failed first semester in Computer Science, I grew discouraged. I ended up switch my degree program - but I never lost my interest in coding. I began to try again - but this time, I would learn on my own. I love the challenges that coding provides: the spark of excitement when you come up with a project idea, the countless hours spent mulling over a simple problem, the drive to go back to work when you walk away in frustration, and the pure joy you feel when your work is complete.
